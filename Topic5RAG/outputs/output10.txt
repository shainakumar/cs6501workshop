Question: What causes a noisy time gear?

Answer: A noisy time gear can be caused by several factors including:

- Excessively worn gears,
- Improperly fitted gears,
- Foreign matter getting between gears and creating a burr on the teeth,
- Failure to sufficiently tighten the camshaft gear lock nut,
- A sprung camshaft or badly worn camshaft front bearing.

Additionally, noises attributed to time gears might also be related to issues such as the generator drive pinion not meshing properly with the large time gear. 

It's important to inspect and possibly replace the gears if they show signs of wear or damage, especially after extended periods of use. If the problem persists, further diagnostic steps may involve checking other components like the commutator and removing the generator to isolate potential faults.
